# Planning & Coordination

### 1. Vocabulary

#### Prioritization & Decision-Making

##### trade-off

balancing two competing advantages

“There’s a **trade-off** between delivery speed and data quality.”

##### deprioritize: reduce importance temporarily

“We may need to deprioritize the reporting module.”

##### align on something with someone: share understanding/agreement

“We need to align on this with product and marketing before the rollout.”

##### set expectations: clarify what people should expect

“We should set expectations early regarding possible delays.”

optional: set + adj + expectations

##### keep someone in the loop: continue updating someone

“Please keep sales in the loop on timeline changes.”

##### over-communicate: communicate very frequently to avoid confusion

“During rollout, it’s better to over-communicate than under-communicate.”

##### take a phased approach/approach this in phases: completing work in stages

“We should approach this item in phases instead of launching everything at once.”

##### map dependency: identifying connected tasks/systems

“We need to identify and map dependencies before development starts.”

##### technical debt: shortcuts in development causing future problems

“If we rush this, we’ll accumulate technical debt.”

##### fallback plan: backup solution

“What’s our fallback plan if the integration fails?”

##### feels aggressive

feels overwhelming, unachievable

“From an engineering standpoint, that timeline feels **aggressive**.”

##### slip

be delayed or miss the planned schedule

“What’s the impact if this **slips** by one week?”

##### optimize for

prioritize or design around a specific goal

“We should **optimize for** stability rather than speed.”

##### must-haves/nice-to-haves

essential requirements or necessary features
optional features that are helpful but not essential

“Let’s separate the **must-haves** from the **nice-to-haves**.”

##### under constraints

working with limitations such as time, budget, or resources

“We’re already operating **under constraints**.”

##### have more visibility

be known by more people

“Let’s revisit this once we **have more visibility**.”
